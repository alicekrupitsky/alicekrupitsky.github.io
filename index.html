About

Hi, I’m Alice Krupitsky, and I am a third-year student at the University of Florida studying Advertising with a minor in Computer Science and an AI Fundamentals Certificate. I’m instinctively drawn to creativity, while also deeply interested in the technology and data behind the scenes.

I grew up in a Russian household in Tampa, which naturally shaped my curiosity about culture, language, and the ways communication influences how people think and connect. I’ve always loved that no two people share the exact same perspective. I see it as an opportunity to explore, learn, and better understand how meaning is created.

I’ve also always been a bit of a data junkie. From a young age, I was drawn to patterns and the way they line up to form trends. When I was younger, I would watch fashion shows with the specific intention of identifying emerging trends. Being able to analyze data using technology feels like a privilege that I’m genuinely grateful for.

Outside of work and school, my interests range from psychology, language learning, fashion (from observing to creating), philosophy, coding, graphic design, reading, writing, weight lifting, swimming, and going on runs. I’m always interested in a little bit of everything, and I don’t see that as something to outgrow.

My long-term goal is to build a career that blends brand strategy, content creation, and technology. My advertising philosophy centers on data-driven creativity: while intuition can spark ideas, meaningful communication is strongest when the art of storytelling is supported by analytics and intentional strategy.
